Evidence-Based Language Learning

The method behind Expatly isn't something we just made up — it's grounded in established second-language-acquisition research and in what actually works, again and again, in my own teaching.

undefined undefined

Shadowing activates your speaking center (Broca's area) far more than plain listening, which only uses your comprehension center (Wernicke's area). That's why you can read the menu but freeze the second you have to talk to the waiter. In my own lessons I see it firsthand: learners who shadow for 15 minutes daily speak noticeably more fluently within a few weeks.

undefined undefined

While your mouth is speaking, your brain quietly locks in grammar patterns along the way — no rule tables required. Linguist Shuhei Kadota describes exactly this effect: grammar is learned through use, not through drilling (Kadota, 2019).

undefined undefined

Speaking isn't just a skill problem — it's a fear problem. Research on shadowing shows learners don't just improve, they genuinely enjoy speaking more. I see the same thing with my own students: the fear fades once the mouth has already said the words a few times.

Not grammar tables. Not perfect scores. Just you, speaking — without the fear standing in your way.
